Queens World Film Festival: the Spanish language films to watch.

The 12th annual edition of the Queens World Film Festival is taking place in person from 1-6 of November with a later online opportunity to enjoy the films from November 20th to December 4th.

words by Maria D. Martinez | Tuesday, Oct 28th, 2022


The yearly festival, which takes place in the NYC borough of Queens, is set to be screening in-person at Queens Theatre, Flushing Town Hall, Museum of the Moving Image, Kaufman Astoria Studios, and The Local. There will also be an online screening through FilmFestivalFlix.com from November 20th – December 4th.

The festival is returning to a thematic programming, grouping films together to create, as they describe it: “specific cinematic experiences'. Saturday 5th is dedicated to films in Spanish language with titles representing Mexico, Cuba and Spain. But the festival is also screening some other movies in Spanish out of this category coming from the US and Dominican Republic.

The fact that the event is hosted in Queens increases the linguistic importance behind it. The native Spanish-speaking community in the borough is the highest in NYC with a 33% of Latinx population according to 2019 data by NYC Gov.However, in total, there will only be 11 titles in Spanish within the nearly 160 movies presented at the festival.

Still, here is a list of the not to miss Spanish language films that will be showcased:

1. Yo, Diablo | Balthazar Klarwein

Spain, 2021, 20 minutes

Joey, works as a waiter for a hedonistic community of summer holiday makers in a small Mediterranean paradise. It is unclear if their exaggerated behaviors are due to the fact that the summer is coming to an end or if its just the last of their summers.

2. Al Final de la Calle | Victor Grullón

Dominican Republic, 2022, 16 minutes

Rafael “El Caballo” García is a professional baseball player who has just lost the most important game of his career. When his mother dies, he has to return to the town where he grew up and face the ghosts of his past. As if this were not enough, he finds out that he has been suspended for the use of steroids and this puts his baseball future in doubt.

3. Monólogo | Pilar García Elegido

Spain, 2021, 15 minutes

“I was not born in the wrong body, I was born in the wrong society.” Josseline Aguilar, a Mexican activist, tells her story and also that of other trans women through a monologue.

4. Detrás de mis Ojos | Ivan Acosta

United States, 40 minutes

Iván M. Acosta witnessed Sept 11 2001 from his balcony in Hell’s Kitchen in West Manhattan. He took out his camera from Super8 and for hours he recorded the worst attack in the history of the city that welcomed him when he emigrated from Cuba. The film remained in storage for almost two decades and today it links them together in a small tribute to New York, the first-line first responders, and all the people who supported and helped each other.

5. Rosa Rosae | Carlos Saura

Spain, 2021, 6 minutes

Saura creates and recovers more than thirty images, drawings and photos that he prints, manipulates, plays with and then films, to create a story that, recreates the Spanish civil war, but also reflects the horrors of a universal war, from the gaze of a child and its environment.

6. Basura | Lorenzo Regalado

Cuba, 1987, 17 minutes

In world of bureaucrats an important dossier got mix-up with a garbage disposal.

7. Novena | Fernando Lopez

United States, 15 minutes

Set in South East Los Angeles, two estranged highschool best-friends reunite at the novenario ceremony for one of their deceased mothers. Over one night in their hometown, feelings resurface and past dynamics are bent in this slice of life narrative that explores the layers / intersections of machismo, culture and religion.

8. Suelta | Javier Pereira

Spain, 2022, 20 minutes

In this narrative short story, the protagonist, Alba, wakes up one morning with two men. Something happened the night before, but she doesn’t remember anything. Just a strange feeling that she can’t define. (The directo Javier Pereira has a prolific career in film, television, and theater, from pre-production to distribution, is multi-awarded at the Malaga Festival, and earns him the Goya for Best New Actor in 2013).

9. Sorda | Nuria Muñoz-Ortín, Eva Libertad

Spain, 2021, 18 minutes

Angela is deaf and Darío is hearing. They are a couple and have six chickens, four dogs, a vegetable garden and many communication problems. Now they are also going to have a child.

10. Anima Animae Animam | José (Putxa) Puchades Martínez, Julieta Gasroc

Spain, 2021, 28 minutes

It is a tribute to the persons who dedicated their lives to sharing their soul with beings who never had it.To the people who gave their voices to objects that had so much to say. To tell old forgotten stories. To the puppeteers, who due to their profession, were not allowed to be buried in cemeteries, as they were considered soulless.

11. Un día de mierda | Vanesa Romero

Spain, 2022, 10 minutes

A shitty day narrates one of the most important days for Leti, where she is left absolutely devastated after her boyfriend stood her up at the altar. Leti wanders on the road and suddenly meets Noelia, a pilgrim on St. James Way. On the way, both women end up finding out what really a shitty day is.

12. Bésame | David Barba

Mexico, 2022, 10 minutes

A mischievous teen explores an empty beach house unaware of the video cameras watching his every move.
